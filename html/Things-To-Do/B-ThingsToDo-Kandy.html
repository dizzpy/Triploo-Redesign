Hiking and Trekking In Knuckles Mountain Range


  • There are many hiking trails in the Knuckles Range, which offer stunning views and landscapes.
  • Pass through dense forests and open plains and take a refreshing dip in a stream or waterfall.
  • Knuckles is home to a rich ecosystem of birds, amphibians, mammals, reptiles, amazing flora and trees.
  • An unforgettable and unique experience is the possibility of hiking with a night camping under the stars.
  • Popular trails are the Mini Worlds End Trail, Dothalugala Nature Trail, Nitro Caves trek and Babarella Hike of 5 Peaks.
  • The entire trek takes approximately 5 to 6 hours to complete so it is advisable to start the journey as early as possible.
  • The best time to climb Knuckles is from February to September.
  • Location: Sri Lanka Knuckles Trekking, No 142/2 Barigama, Werellagma, 20000

Visit The Temple Of The Scared Tooth Relic


  • The tooth, which was originally buried in several temples around the island, was brought to Ceylon in the 4th century.
  • The golden casket is encrusted with seven precious stones and is mounted on an elephant in the annual procession.
  • Due to the absence of a king during the British rule, the guardianship of Dalada was entrusted to Buddhist monks.
  • Religious services, poojas, are held daily at dawn, midday and in the evening and can be viewed by visitors. The services are accompanied with traditional music and drumming.
  • The entire temple complex is built in upland classical architecture.
  • The interior of the temple is decorated with exquisite and amazing paintings.
  • By going there, you can see and worship them.
  • Location: Sri Dalada Veediya, Kandy 20000 , Sri Lanka
  • Opening times:5:30 am – 8:00 pm
  • Entrance fee : Foreign Nationals is Rs. 1500/-
  • Dress code : should be modest with shoulders and knees been covered.Footwear will have to be removed.

Visit To Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage


  • A home for orphaned and injured elephants was found in the wild and rescued.
  • This institute was established in 1975 by the Sri Lanka Wildlife Department.
  • Connect with them by feeding them with large bottles of milk that can be done at regular times.
  • Watching these elephants swim and play in the river is a fascinating experience.
  • Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage has attracted the attention and interest of many animal activists and scientists around the world.
  • Many people love this attraction, making it a very popular place as you can get up close and personal with the majestic mammals.
  • Location: Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage, Rambukkana
  • Opening times: 8:30am¬-5:45pm
