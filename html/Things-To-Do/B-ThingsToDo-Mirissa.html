Whale Watching in Mirissa


  • Mirissa is closest to the continental shelf at Dondra point which is on the migration path of whales and dolphins.
  • Spot the unique breed of blue whales â€“ the largest mammal of the ocean.
  • Sperm whales, killer and pilot whales are species can be spotted.
  • Enjoy a spectacle of dancing dolphins that pop up and dive, showing off for you.
  • In addition of exciting sights of whales and dolphins, encounter views of stunning sunrises.
  • Best time to visit: November to April

Surfing in Mirissa


  • The gorgeous beach of Mirissa has great waves perfect for surfing.
  • You can surf a low power right-hander over reef break near the rocks and a left-hander near the Mirissa harbour.
  • Ideal waves at the beach break point for beginner surfing and boogie boarding.
  • Quieter atmosphere and less crowded.
  • Best time for surfing:November to March

The Dondra Head Lighthouse


  • One of the iconic historical landmarks in the southern district would be the Dondra Head Lighthouse, located on the southern most tip of the island.
  • It is a white octagonal brick tower with a height of 161m and is one of the oldest kept lighthouses.
  • The view is fantastic as you get a view of the crystal clear ocean and a panoramic view of the bay of Matara lined with palm trees.
