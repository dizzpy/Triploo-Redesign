Explore Sri Lanka

Trincomalee

Trincomalee is a city located on the northeastern coast of Sri Lanka. It is known for its natural harbor, which is one of the largest and deepest in the world, making it a strategically significant location historically. Trincomalee is a diverse and culturally rich city with a mix of ethnic groups, including Sinhalese, Tamils, and Muslims.

Temple Of Tooth

The Temple Of The Tooth, also known as Sri Dalada Maligawa, is a sacred Buddhist temple located in Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is one of the most important religious and cultural sites in the country. The temple houses a relic believed to be a tooth of Lord Buddha, which is enshrined in a golden stupa or dagoba.

Galle Fort

Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Galle, Sri Lanka, is a 16th-century Portuguese fortification built by the Dutch. Known for its well-preserved architecture and blend of European and South Asian influences, it is a popular tourist destination.

Jaffna

Jaffna is a city located in the northern part of Sri Lanka. It is the capital of the Jaffna District and is known for its rich cultural and historical significance. The city has a predominantly Tamil population and is considered a major cultural and political center for the Tamil people in Sri Lanka.

Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura is an ancient city located in Sri Lanka. It was the first capital of the country and is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Anuradhapura served as the political and religious center of Sri Lanka for many centuries, starting around the 4th century BCE.

Sigiriya Rock

Sigiriya Rock, also known as the "Lion Rock," is a famous archaeological site in Sri Lanka. It is a massive rock formation that rises nearly 200 meters (660 feet) above the surrounding landscape. Sigiriya is renowned for its historical and cultural significance, as well as its stunning natural beauty.
